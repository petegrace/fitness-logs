Get started now!

Log yourself in, or if you're a new user simply use your existing Google account to sign yourself up and start using Training Ticks straight away. Or if you need some convincing on why you should use Training Ticks then scroll down to check the features available to enhance your training and help keep yourself motivated!

Record cross-training exercises with just one click.

Training Ticks gives you an easy-to-use tool for tracking your strengthening and stretching exercises, or whatever other activities you like to include in your training. Once you've logged an exercise type once it becomes available as a new button to record completed exercises based on how you normally do them.

If you've already recorded your activity in Strava then just Connect With Strava to see all of your training in one place.

Deeper insight on your Strava activities.

If your Strava activity includes cadence data then Training Ticks will import it and provde you with insightful visualisations so you can understand your natural cadence and how it changes from activity to activity. Great if you want to try and improve your leg speed to get that extra performance benefit.

Analyse your activity for the week.

The Analysis & Goals section lets you see all of your activity for the week in one place under a simple, expandable interface. Charts at the top aggregate your data up to a weekly level and allow you to set and track goals.

Create your own training plan.

Schedule your preferred exercises across a recurring weekly training plan so that you're prompted with your exercises to do each day.

Set and track weekly goals.

You can set for cadence and completed exercise sets (more options coming soon!) simply by clicking within the charts, either for the current or upcoming week. Your progress for all goals you have each week is then easily visibly from the same page.

Categorisation and colour-coding.

Set your own categories for activities and exercises to give yourself a more user-friendly interface through colour-coding of activities, and allow you to segment your activities when analysing.
